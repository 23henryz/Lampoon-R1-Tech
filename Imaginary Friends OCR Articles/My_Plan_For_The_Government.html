My Plan for the Government

The government could stand to be a little less serious. Have you ever tried telling the government a joke? Most of the time, it doesn’t even laugh. Most of the time it just keeps on chugging along the complex workings of a giant bureaucracy. One time I totally killed it with my Borat impression though. Here is a typical attempt to tell a killer joke to the normal, boring, government:

Me: Knock knock.

Government: To call DMV, press 1, to call the Mayor’s Office, press 2, to hear this in Spanish…

Me: Interrupting Cow.

Government: ¡Para llamar al DMV, presione 1, para llamar a la Oficina del Alcalde, presione 2, para escuchar estas instrucciones nuevamente en inglés!

Me: [simultaneously] Moooooooooo.

Government: Eh.

I’m sorry, I’m a person trying to make jokes, which increases joy, which I thought was supposed to be the goal of the government in the first place. Turns out, the goal of the government is to give you a bunch of paperwork so they can collect your signature in case you get famous.

In my view, the government should try to be more funny. What if the IRS, instead of selling your house and recouping their losses exactly, lit your pants on fire, shit in your wallet, and made your uncle handicapped? This would make life, which can be quite dreary, remarkably more interesting. In the ancient days, when the government was mad at you, they’d just bang your wife and bury you halfway in the ground while throwing stones at your face. This is hilarious.
